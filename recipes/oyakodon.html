Description

Oyakodon is a very simple recipe. Its flavour combines both savouriness and sweetness! It consists of a chicken and egg mixture, placed on top of steamed rice.

"Oya" (親) means "parent" and "ko" (子) means "child", referring to the chicken and the egg, respectively. "Don" (丼) means "(rice) bowl" and is used to refer to a variety of Japanese recipes which feature toppings served over rice. Other famous examples are Gyuudon (beef rice bowl), Katsudon (deep-fried pork cutlet rice bowl) and Unadon (Eel rice bowl).

Ingredients

  • ?g chicken
  • 2 eggs
  • ?g onions
  • Optional: mushrooms, or other toppings of choice
  • ?g dashi powder
  • 100ml water
  • ?ml mirin
  • ?ml soy sauce
  • Oil
  • Cooked rice

Steps

  1. Pre-heat the pan and add oil.
  2. Cut chicken into bite size pieces, slice onions into long strips. Cut up the optional toppings into bite-sized pieces.
  3. Start frying by adding the onions first, then the chicken. (Add the optional toppings)
  4. In a bowl, beat the eggs and combine with dashi, water, mirin and soy sauce.
  5. Reduce heat once chicken is cooked through and pour egg mixture over chicken and onions. Cover with lid and let simmer
  6. Turn off heat once eggs are half-cooked, they should still be runny.
  7. Arrange chicken and egg mixture on top of rice.
