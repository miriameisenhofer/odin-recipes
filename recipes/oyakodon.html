Description

Oyakodon is a very simple recipe. Its flavour combines both savouriness and sweetness! It consists of a chicken and egg mixture, placed on top of steamed rice.

"Oya" (親) means "parent" and "ko" (子) means "child", referring to the chicken and the egg, respectively. "Don" (丼) means "(rice) bowl" and is used to refer to a variety of Japanese recipes which feature toppings served over rice. Other famous examples are Gyuudon (beef rice bowl), Katsudon (deep-fried pork cutlet rice bowl) and Unadon (Eel rice bowl).
